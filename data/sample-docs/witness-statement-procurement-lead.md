# File note — Witness interview A. Berger

**Preliminary note by the auditors:** Ms. Berger was informed about the
purpose and the voluntary nature of the interview. This is not an
employment-law hearing. Statements will be treated confidentially.

**Question:** Ms. Berger, do you know the company Adriatic Advisory d.o.o.?

**Answer:** Yes, by name. The company appears in our vendor master data
for the SEE region. I never engaged them myself.

**Question:** Who negotiated the contract with Adriatic Advisory?

**Answer:** As far as I know, Mr. Petrović directly. I was told that it
was being run "through the sales line", not through procurement. I found
that unusual at the time because advisory services normally go through us.

**Question:** Have you seen any deliverables from Adriatic Advisory —
reports, presentations, market analyses?

**Answer:** No. I asked once — that was autumn 2025 — and directly asked
Mr. Petrović for a copy of the advisory deliverables because I needed
comparable market data for another tender. I never received anything.
The answer was: "These are oral advisory services, there are no reports."

**Question:** Do you find it plausible — EUR 740,000 for purely oral
advisory over 14 months?

**Answer:** Honestly, no. With other consultants of this size we always
receive at least a monthly report and a closing presentation per project
phase.

**Question:** Are you aware of any personal connections between
Mr. Petrović and Adriatic Advisory?

**Answer:** There are rumours in the team that the CEO of Adriatic,
Mr. Toni Marić, is a former university friend of Mr. Petrović. I can't
prove that, however. I have never checked it myself.

**Question:** Who else would have had visibility into the matter?

**Answer:** Ms. Klein in Accounting approved the invoices. She probably
only reviewed them formally because the instruction came from above. And
Marko Schneider from Internal Audit — he started asking questions and
then came to Dr. Bauer with his observations.

**Closing remark:** Ms. Berger states that she is willing to provide
further information, provided that employment-law protection is in place.
She notes that Mr. Petrović is "very well connected" and that she does
not dare raise the matter publicly.
