# Group policy BR-COM-02 — Anti-Corruption and Gifts (excerpt)

## §2 Scope

This policy applies to all employees, members of corporate bodies and any
other persons acting on behalf of Helios worldwide. It supplements the
applicable statutory requirements (in Germany in particular §§ 299, 331
ff. StGB, IntBestG, OWiG).

## §4 Prohibition of bribery and granting of advantages

It is strictly prohibited to grant, offer or promise advantages — directly
or indirectly via third parties (in particular consultants, agents,
intermediaries) — to public officials or business partners in the course
of business, where this is capable of unduly influencing a business
decision. **Indirect payments via consultants are subject to the same
restrictions as direct payments.**

## §7 Engagement of external consultants (third-party risk)

Engaging external consultants in connection with business development —
in particular in the public sector and in high-risk countries (see
Annex A: Risk-Country List) — is only permissible under the following
conditions:

1. **Due diligence:** Written third-party due diligence (identity,
   beneficial ownership, reputational research).
2. **Written contract:** Concrete description of services, market-rate
   remuneration, reporting obligations, audit rights, compliance clause
   with termination right in the event of breach.
3. **Four-eyes principle:** For a volume > EUR 100,000 a **second signature
   from the Legal department** is mandatory.
4. **Proof of deliverables:** Payments are only made against documented
   proof of deliverables (reports, presentations, written recommendations).
   *Purely "oral advisory" is not sufficient as proof of deliverables.*
5. **Success fees:** Success fees in the public sector require approval by
   the group management board without exception.

## §9 Reporting obligation and whistleblower protection

Employees who suspect a breach of this policy should contact Compliance
(compliance@helios-...) or the anonymous whistleblower system. Retaliation
against whistleblowers is prohibited (§ 36 HinSchG).

## Annex A — Risk-Country List (excerpt 2024)

High risk (tier 3): Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Kosovo,
Montenegro, North Macedonia, Serbia (application of the enhanced
due-diligence process is mandatory).
