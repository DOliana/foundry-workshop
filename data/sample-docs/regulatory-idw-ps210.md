# IDW PS 210 — paraphrased excerpt for the workshop

## Purpose and application

IDW Auditing Standard 210 specifies, for German statutory audits, the
auditor's obligations regarding *non-compliance with laws and other
regulations* (NOCLAR — Non-Compliance with Laws and Regulations).

## Risk classes

PS 210 distinguishes:

- **Directly relevant provisions:** Provisions whose compliance directly
  affects the amount of items or disclosures in the financial statements
  (e.g. tax law requirements, GoB).
- **Indirectly relevant provisions:** Provisions whose breach does not
  directly affect the financial statements, but whose compliance is
  essential for the continued operation of the business (e.g.
  anti-corruption, anti-money-laundering, sanctions law, LkSG).

## Obligations upon initial suspicion

When an initial suspicion of a legal violation arises, the auditor shall:

1. Understand the facts of the case, including the persons involved and
   the chronological sequence;
2. Classify the facts legally — *which concrete offences* may apply
   (e.g. § 299 StGB, § 334 StGB i. V. m. IntBestG, § 30 OWiG / § 130 OWiG,
   § 261 StGB);
3. Assess the impact on the financial statements and on the reliability
   of the representations made by the legal representatives;
4. Inform management and, where applicable, the supervisory board / audit
   committee — observing the *escalation thresholds* under §§27–31;
5. Examine whether there is a reporting obligation to supervisory
   authorities (in particular § 32 KWG, § 4d FinDAG, § 53 GwG, depending
   on the sector);
6. Document the measures taken and the conclusions in an *audit-proof*
   manner.

## Initial Assessment Memo

In audit practice, the **Initial Assessment Memo** has become established
as a structured format for the initial documentation of a suspected case.
The memo records:

- Facts of the case (factual, with sources);
- Persons involved and their functions;
- Legal assessment (which provisions are potentially violated);
- Assessment of materiality for the financial statements;
- Required next steps (interviews, data analysis, external advice);
- Escalation decision and recipients.
